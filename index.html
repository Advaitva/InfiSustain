Past

As a fossil fuel burned for heat, coal still supplies about a quarter of the world's energy. It is the largest anthropogenic source of carbon dioxide amounting to about 40% of the total fossil fuel emissions. As part of the worldwide energy transition, many countries have stopped using or use less coal but still a large chunk of global energy requirement remains dependent on coal.

Coal

Petroleum is still the primary source of energy for majority of the countries. The entire planet has suffered degradation due to humanity's overuse of fossil fuels. Toxic substances released by petroleum not only damage the environment but also impacts our health. There are also concerns like oil spills that have a very negative impact on marine life.

Petroleum
Present

Nuclear power supplies about 10% of the global electricity and is the second largest low carbon power source after hydroelectricity. It has one of the lowest levels of fatalities per unit of energy generated. When used safely, it is one of the best low emission energy sources.

Nuclear

Wind energy is one of the best sustainable and renewable source of energy and has a much smaller impact on the environment compared to burning fossil fuels. Onshore wind is an inexpensive source of electric power, competitive with or in many places cheaper than coal or gas plant.

Wind

The sun provides enough energy in an hour to fulfill global energy needs for a year. It is the best inexhaustable source of clean energy and is important in our transition from conventional energy sources. The limitation of solar power is our ability to turn it onto electricity in an efficient and cost effective way.

Solar

Hydropower currenty generates about 16% of the worlds total energy and 70% of all renewable electricity. The cost of hydroelectric is relatively low, making it a competitive option. Once a hydroelectric complex is constructed, the project produces almost no direct waste and only a tiny amount of emission.

Hydoelectric

Geothermal power is cost-effective, reliable, sustainable, and environmentally friendly,but has historically been limited to areas near tectonic plate boundaries. However, recent technological advances have dramatically expanded the range and size of viable resources.

Geothermal

Burning plant derived biomass releases CO2 but it has still been classified as a renewable energy source because photosyntheses cycles the CO2 bac into new crops. In some cases, this recycling of CO2 from plans to atmosplere back into plants can even by CO2 negative.

Biomass

    • Future

    Fossil fuels like coal, oil and natural gas supply almost 80% of the world's energy and are the primary human source of greenhouse gas emissions. Even pretending for a moment that climate change doesnt matter and completely ignoring greenhouse effect, global temperature effect, ocean acidification and sea-level rise, it becomes clear from a long term point of view that we still need a plan for our energy future. Fossil fuels are fundamentally limited resources and will be gone sooner or later. Renewable sources like wind, solar and hydroelectric power have a different limitation-they are inconsistent. Therefore. the only long term solution comes from sources like nuclear fusion, but till the time technology is not feasable, we need to transition away from conventional sources as soon as possible to minimize the harm done to our planet.
